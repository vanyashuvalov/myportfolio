## Project Overview

Biny is the first freight-tech and deep-tech startup from Kazakhstan — a digital ecosystem for logistics management that simplifies routine work for shippers, carriers, and dispatchers. Their mission is to make Central Asian logistics transparent and accessible.

The driver app is one of four products in the ecosystem:
- Web app for shippers
- Web app for dispatchers
- Mobile app for drivers
- AI-powered pricing algorithm

::: callout info
💡 **Impact**: 200+ screens designed, 50+ research sessions, 20+ updates over a year
:::

## The Challenge

Drivers in Central Asia typically work using simple, traditional methods: phone calls, WhatsApp, and paper notes. Transitioning them to a digital product presented unique challenges:

- **Digital Literacy Gap**: Target users are 50-60 years old with limited computer experience
- **Trust Issues**: New users often mistook the app for a scam due to unfamiliar digital payment systems
- **Language Barriers**: Most users are Kazakh speakers, requiring native interface support
- **Connectivity Issues**: Rural areas have poor network coverage, making offline access critical
- **Support Overload**: Every driver called support constantly, not just from confusion but from habit

### Original App Problems

Based on our audit of the existing MVP:

- Complex multi-step registration流程容易迷路
- Missing basic features: order history, support, advanced filters and sorting
- Text errors and unclear copy
- No interface feedback (no animations, status updates, or event notifications)
- Web-based app unavailable offline in slow network areas

## Research & Discovery

### User Interviews

I spoke with **10 drivers over 3 days** in the field, observing their actual workflows and pain points:

::: quote Experienced Driver, Almaty Region
I've been driving for 30 years. This phone app thing is completely new to me. I prefer calling someone who can explain what to do.
:::

### Key Insights

1. **Low Digital Literacy**: Average user is a 55-year-old long-haul driver who prefers offline activities
2. **Native Language Need**: Most users are Kazakh speakers requiring interface in their native language
3. **Trust Building Critical**: New users need clear explanations of how payments and transactions work
4. **Support Dependency**: Moderators were overwhelmed, compensating for all UX deficiencies

### Competitive Analysis

Studied other logistics and delivery apps in the region to identify best practices for driver-focused interfaces.

## Design Solution

### Design Goals (Prioritized)

1. **Simplify Registration Flow**: Original completion rate was extremely low — if drivers couldn't register, nothing else mattered
2. **Add In-App Guidance**: High impact with minimal development effort
3. **Streamline Order Workflow**: App should be a guide and tool, not a problem requiring moderator intervention
4. **Implement Notification System**: At minimum, inform users of status changes; at best, re-engage them with orders

### Information Architecture

Redesigned user flow to ensure:

- Clear, linear registration process
- One primary action per screen (reduced to **1 button** in key moments)
- Native language throughout
- Offline-first capability
- Trust-building payment explanations

### Visual Design System

Created driver-friendly interface featuring:

- **Large, Minimal Elements**: Easy to tap and read
- **Color-Coded Statuses**: Clear visual hierarchy for order states
- **Step-by-Step Onboarding**: Guided walkthrough for new users
- **Illustration Support**: Custom graphics explaining complex concepts
- **High Contrast**: Readable in various lighting conditions (daylight, night driving)

### Key Features

#### 1. Simplified Registration

- Reduced steps from 12 to 4
- Clear progress indicators
- Document upload with camera integration
- Real-time validation feedback
- Moderation status notifications

#### 2. In-App Training

- Interactive onboarding for new features
- Tooltips for complex actions
- Video tutorials for common tasks
- FAQ section with search

#### 3. Order Management

- Simple order search with basic filters
- One-tap order acceptance
- Clear status progression
- Push notifications for status changes
- Offline order caching

#### 4. Payment & Transactions

- Clear payment status visibility
- Step-by-step withdrawal process
- Transaction history with filters
- Bank transfer instructions
- Support chat for payment issues

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked Well

1. **Contextual Research**: Meeting drivers in their environment revealed real pain points
2. **Minimal Interface**: Reducing to one primary action dramatically improved task completion
3. **Native Language**: Kazakh language support was critical for adoption
4. **Iterative Updates**: 20+ updates allowed continuous improvement

### Challenges Overcome

1. **Trust Building**: Clear payment explanations reduced fraud concerns
2. **Digital Literacy**: Training modules helped older users adapt
3. **Offline Access**: Caching orders for poor connectivity areas
4. **Stakeholder Alignment**: Balancing speed with quality under tight deadlines

### Future Improvements

- AI-powered order recommendations
- Voice commands for hands-free operation
- Integration with truck telemetry
- Multi-language support (Russian, Uzbek, Kyrgyz)
- Driver rating and rewards system

## Conclusion

The Biny Driver App project demonstrates how thoughtful UX design can bridge the gap between traditional workflows and digital transformation. By deeply understanding the target audience — experienced drivers with limited digital experience — we created a solution that earned their trust and significantly reduced support burden.

The key insight: **simplicity wins**. By reducing interface complexity and providing clear guidance, even users skeptical of technology can successfully adopt digital tools.
